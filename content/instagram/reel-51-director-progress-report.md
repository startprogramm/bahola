## Concept

A school director opens the TezTekshir director dashboard on a tablet. We follow their eyes as they scan: class performance charts, grade distributions, which classes are improving, where weak spots are. No chaos, no spreadsheets — just clean data, instantly readable. They tap into one class, see submission rates, average score trend over 4 weeks. They close the tablet with confidence. Tone: calm authority. This is what informed leadership looks like.

Audience: teachers who want to impress their director, and directors who don't yet know this dashboard exists.

## Music Mood

Executive, calm, confident. Think: documentary score, minimal piano or clean synth. Not fast — deliberate. The pace matches a leader who already knows what they're doing. No drama, just clarity.

---

## Production Notes

- **Audience is dual.** Teachers watching will think "my director should see this." Directors watching will think "I need this." Write the visuals for both.
- **Use real dashboard UI.** The director dashboard must show actual TezTekshir data — not mockups. Charts, submission rates, score distributions. If needed, use anonymized test data with realistic numbers.
- **The director is calm, not excited.** Excitement suggests novelty. Calm confidence suggests this is just how things work now. That's the aspiration.
- **Shot 4 is the differentiator.** Most tools show averages. TezTekshir shows individual weak spots. This is the detail that impresses directors — they don't just want class averages, they want to know which students need intervention.
- **Tablet > phone for this reel.** The director dashboard should be shown on a tablet or laptop — not a phone. It signals executive context, and the larger screen shows more data at once.
- **No voiceover.** Text overlays carry the message. Music sets the tone.
- **Duration: 28–33 seconds.** Longer than Reel 50 is fine — this concept benefits from a slightly slower, more deliberate pace.
- **Shot 5 (tablet close):** This is the emotional resolve. Don't skip it. "Data told the story, no meeting needed" is the director's fantasy. Sell it with the physical gesture — cover closes, lean back, done.
- **Russian subtitle option:** Given directors may be Russian-speaking, consider adding a brief Russian caption block if the campaign supports bilingual posting.
