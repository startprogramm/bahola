**Duration:** 30–45 seconds

## Concept

Fast-cut workflow demo: teacher uploads an entire class worth of student work and watches Bahola grade all of them — in under 2 minutes. Clock visible throughout. No narration, just the screen, the timer, and the result counter climbing. Ends on the final score summary with every student graded. Tone: satisfying, almost hypnotic. Like watching a speed-run record get broken.

The hook is the clock. The payoff is "30 students. 1 min 47 sec. Done."

## Music Mood

Upbeat, rhythmic, escalating. Think: game speedrun BGM or competitive cooking show music. Each graded result should feel like a satisfying hit. Climax at the 1:47 reveal, then resolution.

---

## Production Notes

- **The clock is the star.** Keep a visible running timer in the corner from the moment grading starts. The 1:47 freeze at the end is the emotional payoff — make it big and bold.
- **Live counter matters.** The "0 → 30 students graded" counter climbing in real time is what makes this feel real. Use the actual Bahola grading queue UI — do not fake it.
- **30 students is the benchmark.** This is a realistic class size for Uzbek schools. Viewers should immediately map "30 ta ish" to their own class.
- **Show actual feedback, not just scores.** Shot 5 is crucial — it proves Bahola doesn't just assign numbers, it writes individual feedback per student. This differentiates from a simple grading script.
- **No voiceover.** The speed and the numbers carry the story. Music + text overlays only.
- **Upload sequence (Shot 2):** Show the actual gallery multi-select — this is a common UX moment teachers will recognize and wonder "can I actually do that?" Yes, they can.
- **Tone:** Don't oversell. The numbers speak. Teacher's slight smile at the end is the only emotional beat you need — understated is more believable.
- **Duration target:** 35–40 seconds. Speed run concept breaks if you go too long.
