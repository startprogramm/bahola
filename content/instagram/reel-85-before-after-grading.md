# Reel 85 — Before vs After: Old Grading vs Bulk Grading Workflow

## Concept
A stark split-screen contrast. Left side: the old way — teacher hunched over a desk, stack of papers, red pen, clock ticking. Right side: the same teacher, phone in hand, TezTekshir open, done before the old-way teacher even finishes the third paper. The contrast is the content. No argument needed — the images speak for themselves. Target: teachers still grading by hand who haven't fully committed to TezTekshir's bulk workflow.

Split screen appears instantly.
Left label: **❌ ILGARI / РАНЬШЕ**
Right label: **✅ HOZIR / СЕЙЧАС**

## Production Notes
- **Split screen must be simultaneous throughout** — both sides run at the same wall-clock time. This is what makes the contrast undeniable.
- Use the same actor for both sides — same teacher, different "reality." Costume can match; hairstyle/expression conveys state.
- Left side color grade: slightly warm, slightly desaturated — old world, tiring.
- Right side color grade: clean, cool, bright — modern, effortless.
- The clock on the wall is the through-line proof — it must be clearly visible and synchronized.
- The stat card comparison at [33–42s] should appear as animated cards sliding in — not static text dump.
- Audio: left side — heavy pen scratching, paper rustle, slow ticking clock / right side — light tap sounds, brief processing hum, then gentle success chime. Mix both channels simultaneously.
- Aspect ratio: 9:16 (Reels)
- Duration: 43–47 seconds
