## Concept

Split-screen comparison: the exact same student answer graded manually vs graded by Bahola AI. Left side shows a teacher with a red pen, vague feedback, one number scrawled. Right side shows Bahola's detailed breakdown — criterion-by-criterion, specific suggestions, in the student's language. Same answer. Completely different experience. Tone: matter-of-fact, not condescending. Let the contrast speak.

## Production Notes

- **Split-screen is the hero format here.** Left side: warm red tones, slightly chaotic energy. Right side: clean teal/white, structured. Let the visual design do the comparison work.
- **The key beat is Shot 4:** the zoom into actual criterion-level feedback. This is what differentiates Bahola. The specific line shown ("Argument: 3/4 — Fikringiz kuchli, lekin misol yetishmaydi") should look like a real output — screenshot or very realistic mockup.
- **Shot 2 intentionally shows minimal manual feedback.** The point isn't that teachers are bad — it's that the system doesn't support rich feedback at scale. Red pen + one number is realistic, not an exaggeration.
- **Shot 5 timing numbers:** "3 soat" is intentionally approximate. "90 soniya" is real — use Bahola's actual grading time from a real session if available.
- **Music:** Starts slightly dull on the manual side → lifts and brightens when AI side appears. Resolution at CTA. Avoid anything that sounds judgmental — this is about capability, not blame.
- **No voiceover needed.** Text overlays and visuals carry the story. Keep it punchy.
- **Duration sweet spot:** 38–42 seconds. The comparison needs room to land without dragging.
