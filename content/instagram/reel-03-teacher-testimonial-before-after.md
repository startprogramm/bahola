# Reel 3: Teacher Before/After (Testimonial Style)

## Hook Line
**"Ilgari: kechqurun 11 da daftarlar bilan. Endi: 6 da — uyga ketish. 🏠"**
*(Before: notebooks at 11pm. Now: home by 6.)*

## Script / Voiceover (testimonial tone — warm, personal)

> **[0–4s]** "Bir vaqtlar kechqurun soat 11 da ham ish stolimdaman edi. 30 ta daftar. Qizil ruchka."
> *(There was a time I was at my desk at 11pm. 30 notebooks. Red pen.)*

> **[4–8s]** "Endi soat 6 da ishni yakunlayman."
> *(Now I finish work at 6.)*

> **[8–14s]** "Bahola har bir o'quvchining javobini o'qiydi, baholaydi va izoh yozadi — men keltirgan mezon asosida."
> *(Bahola reads each student's answer, grades it, and writes feedback — based on the criteria I set.)*

> **[14–22s]** "Izohlar aniq: qaysi fikr to'g'ri aytilmagan, qaysi ball nima uchun berilmagan."
> *(The feedback is specific: which point wasn't made, why marks were lost.)*

> **[22–28s]** "Kerak bo'lsa — men o'zim bir savolni qayta baholayman. Bu 10 sekund."
> *(If needed, I can re-mark a question myself. That takes 10 seconds.)*

> **[28–35s]** "Bahola menga — ustozga — vaqt berdi. O'quvchilarga — adolatli baho."
> *(Bahola gave me — the teacher — my time back. Students got fair grades.)*

> **[35–40s]** "Bepul. Siz ham sinab ko'ring."
> *(Free. Try it yourself.)*

## Director Notes
- Tone: warm, personal — like a teacher talking to a colleague, not a brand pitch
- No actors needed — a real teacher (or someone who looks the part) works best
- The clock detail in the before/after is the emotional anchor — make it clear
- Keep transitions snappy: the before/after cut should feel like a flip
