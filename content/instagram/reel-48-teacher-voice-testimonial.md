# Reel 48: Teacher Voice — Real Words, Real Results

**Slug:** `reel-48-teacher-voice-testimonial`

**Duration:** 45–60 seconds

---

## Concept

Talking-head style reel — a real teacher (or teacher-actor) speaks directly to camera about their experience with TezTekshir. Warm, conversational, authentic. No polished corporate feel. This is a colleague talking to colleagues. Tone: honest, human, slightly emotional. The message: "I was skeptical too — here's what actually happened."

The authenticity of the format is the product of. Real words from a real person carry more weight than any stats graphic.

## Production Notes

- **Authenticity is everything.** If this is a real teacher, capture in their actual classroom or workspace — not a staged studio. If it's a teacher-actor, the setting should still feel genuinely educational (whiteboard, books, student work visible in the background).
- **Camera:** Slightly below eye level, handheld or barely stabilized. Feels like a friend recorded it. Not polished corporate video.
- **Wardrobe:** Normal teacher clothes. No uniforms, no formal dress unless it's what they'd actually wear.
- **Lighting:** Natural window light preferred. Warm, not harsh. No ring light glare if avoidable.
- **The screen recording insert (Shot 3):** Keep it brief — 2–3 seconds max. It's an accent, not a tutorial. Just show the number of submissions processed + the timestamp showing how fast it went.
- **The smile at "Endi har juma":** This is the emotional peak. Don't cut around it. Let it land.
- **Subtitles:** Add Uzbek subtitles throughout. This is a talking-head reel and many users watch without sound.
- **Music:** Warm, intimate. Think lo-fi with a human heartbeat. Should fade almost to nothing during the emotional pivot (Shot 4) to let the silence work.
- **No voiceover needed.** Teacher's direct voice IS the content.
